What Is a Gold IRA?

A Gold IRA (also called a precious metals IRA) is a self-directed Individual Retirement Account that allows you to invest in physical gold, silver, platinum, and palladium instead of traditional paper assets like stocks and bonds. The IRS requires that the metals meet minimum purity standards (99.5% for gold) and be stored at an approved depository.

Gold IRAs come in three varieties:

  • Traditional Gold IRA — Contributions may be tax-deductible. You pay taxes on withdrawals in retirement.
  • Roth Gold IRA — Contributions are made with after-tax dollars. Qualified withdrawals in retirement are tax-free.
  • SEP Gold IRA — Designed for self-employed individuals and small business owners with higher contribution limits.

The key benefit is diversification. When stocks crash, gold has historically held its value or even increased. During the 2008 financial crisis, gold rose 25% while the S&P 500 fell 38%. In 2024-2025, gold hit new all-time highs above $2,700 per ounce as investors fled uncertainty.

How to Rollover Your 401(k) to a Gold IRA

Rolling over a 401(k) or traditional IRA into a Gold IRA is a straightforward process that typically takes 2 to 3 weeks. Here is the step-by-step process:

  1. Choose a Gold IRA company. Based on our comparison above, select the company that best fits your investment amount and priorities.
  2. Open a self-directed IRA. Your chosen company will help you set up a self-directed IRA with an approved custodian (typically Equity Trust, GoldStar Trust, or New Direction IRA).
  3. Initiate the rollover. Your Gold IRA company handles the paperwork to transfer funds from your existing 401(k) or IRA. This is a trustee-to-trustee transfer, meaning you never touch the money and there are no tax penalties.
  4. Select your metals. Work with your specialist to choose IRA-eligible gold, silver, platinum, or palladium products.
  5. Metals are purchased and stored. Your custodian purchases the metals and they are shipped to an IRS-approved depository for secure storage.

Important: A direct rollover (trustee-to-trustee) is not a taxable event. However, if you take a distribution and then deposit the funds yourself, you have 60 days to complete the rollover or face taxes and potential penalties. Always do a direct rollover.

Pros and Cons of Gold IRAs

Pros

  • Inflation hedge: Gold has historically maintained its purchasing power during inflationary periods. As the dollar weakens, gold tends to rise.
  • Portfolio diversification: Gold has low to negative correlation with stocks and bonds, reducing overall portfolio risk.
  • Tax advantages: Same tax benefits as a traditional or Roth IRA — tax-deferred growth or tax-free withdrawals depending on account type.
  • Physical ownership: Unlike gold ETFs, you own actual gold bars and coins stored in secure vaults.
  • Crisis protection: Gold performs well during geopolitical turmoil, banking crises, and market crashes.

Cons

  • Higher fees: Gold IRAs cost more than standard brokerage IRAs due to custodian fees, storage fees, and dealer premiums.
  • No dividends or interest: Gold does not generate income. Your return depends entirely on price appreciation.
  • Higher minimums: Most Gold IRA companies require $10,000 to $50,000 to open an account, versus $0 at many online brokerages.
  • Liquidity concerns: Selling physical gold takes longer than selling stocks or ETFs. Most companies offer buyback programs, but the process is not instant.
  • Storage requirements: IRS rules mandate that gold be stored at an approved depository. You cannot store IRA gold at home.

How Much of Your Portfolio Should Be in Gold?

Most financial advisors recommend allocating 5% to 15% of your total retirement portfolio to precious metals. This provides meaningful diversification without overexposing you to a single asset class.

Consider your risk tolerance and timeline:

  • Conservative investors (near retirement): 10-15% in gold for maximum protection against market downturns.
  • Moderate investors (10-20 years to retirement): 5-10% in gold as a diversification hedge.
  • Aggressive investors (20+ years to retirement): 5% or less in gold, with the rest in growth-oriented assets.

Remember, gold is a long-term hold. It works best when you buy and hold for years or decades, not when you try to time short-term price swings.

Red Flags to Watch For

The precious metals industry has its share of bad actors. Watch out for these warning signs:

  • High-pressure sales tactics: Reputable companies educate first and sell second. If someone is pushing you to act immediately, walk away.
  • No BBB or BCA listing: All legitimate Gold IRA companies should have a BBB profile. Check for complaints.
  • Vague or hidden fees: Ask for a complete fee schedule in writing before committing. Total annual fees should be under $300.
  • Promises of guaranteed returns: Gold prices fluctuate. Anyone guaranteeing returns is being dishonest.
  • Home storage schemes: Some companies claim you can store IRA gold at home in a safe. The IRS requires third-party depository storage. Home storage can trigger taxes, penalties, and account disqualification.

Gold IRA vs. Gold ETF — Which Is Better?

If you have a substantial retirement account and want true physical ownership with tax advantages, a Gold IRA is the better choice. If you want simple, low-cost gold exposure with instant liquidity, a gold ETF in a regular brokerage account works well.
