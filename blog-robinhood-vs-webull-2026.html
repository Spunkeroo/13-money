Robinhood vs Webull 2026 — Which Trading App Is Better?

Updated March 2, 2026 · 13 min read · Back to Dashboard

Robinhood and Webull are the two most popular commission-free trading apps in the United States, and both have evolved significantly since their early days. Robinhood pioneered the zero-commission model that forced the entire brokerage industry to follow. Webull answered with professional-grade charting tools and technical analysis that rival desktop platforms costing hundreds of dollars per month. In 2026, both apps are more capable than ever — but they serve different types of investors.

We tested both platforms extensively, executing real trades across stocks, options, ETFs, and crypto. This is the definitive head-to-head comparison covering every feature that matters: fees, charting, crypto, options, fractional shares, user interface, free stock offers, and the things neither company wants you to know about.

Choose Robinhood if you want the simplest, cleanest investing experience with great cash management features, crypto trading, and a credit card that earns investment rewards.

Choose Webull if you want powerful charting tools, technical indicators, extended trading hours, and a platform that feels like a professional trading terminal.

Choose both — seriously. Both are free. Open both accounts, claim both free stock bonuses, and use whichever fits your workflow. Many active traders use Webull for analysis and Robinhood for execution.

Fees and Commissions

Both Robinhood and Webull offer $0 commission on stocks, ETFs, and options. But "free" does not mean there are no costs. Here is what you actually pay:

Stock and ETF Trades

Both platforms charge $0 commission. However, both make money through payment for order flow (PFOF) — they route your orders to market makers who pay for the right to execute your trades. This can result in slightly worse execution prices compared to direct-access brokers. For most retail investors trading less than $100,000 per year, the price improvement difference is negligible — fractions of a penny per share.

Options Trades

Both charge $0 commission and $0 per contract. This is genuinely free — no hidden per-contract fees like you will find at Schwab ($0.65/contract) or Fidelity ($0.65/contract). For active options traders, this saves real money.

Crypto Trades

Both advertise $0 commission on crypto, but both apply a spread markup — the difference between the buy and sell price. This spread is typically 0.5% to 1.5% depending on the asset and market conditions. Robinhood has been more transparent about this, displaying the spread before you confirm a trade. On Webull, the spread can be slightly wider on less liquid altcoins.

For serious crypto trading, neither Robinhood nor Webull is optimal. Dedicated crypto exchanges like Coinbase Advanced (0.40% maker/0.60% taker) or MEXC (0% fees) are cheaper. Use Robinhood or Webull for small, convenient crypto purchases alongside your stock portfolio.

Premium Subscriptions

Robinhood Gold ($5/month): Includes 4.5% APY on uninvested cash, Level 2 market data (Nasdaq TotalView), lower margin rates (5.75%), higher instant deposit limits ($50K), professional research reports from Morningstar, and the Robinhood Gold credit card (3% cashback).

Webull Premium ($0): Webull does not charge for its advanced features. Level 2 data, advanced charting with 50+ technical indicators, and extended trading hours are all included free. However, Webull does offer Nasdaq TotalView as an add-on for $2.99/month.

Charting and Technical Analysis

This is where Webull dominates. If you care about technical analysis, Webull is the clear winner.

Robinhood Charting

Robinhood's charts are clean and visually appealing but basic. You get candlestick and line charts, a handful of time frames (1D, 1W, 1M, 3M, 1Y, 5Y), and a few basic indicators (SMA, EMA, RSI, MACD, Bollinger Bands). There is no ability to draw trendlines, set alerts on chart levels, or compare multiple securities on one chart. For buy-and-hold investors, this is fine. For active traders, it is limiting.

Webull Charting

Webull's charting suite is legitimately professional-grade. You get 50+ technical indicators including VWAP, Fibonacci retracements, Ichimoku Cloud, and volume profile. Drawing tools include trendlines, channels, horizontal rays, and Fibonacci tools. You can set 12 different chart time frames, compare multiple tickers, use multiple chart layouts, and even run charts in a full-screen desktop mode that rivals TradingView.

Webull also offers a paper trading simulator with $1,000,000 in virtual money. This lets beginners practice without risking real capital — a feature Robinhood completely lacks.

Winner: Webull, by a wide margin.

Crypto Trading

Both platforms support crypto trading, but with important differences.

Robinhood Crypto

Robinhood supports 20+ cryptocurrencies including Bitcoin, Ethereum, Solana, Dogecoin, Cardano, Polygon, and Avalanche. You can transfer crypto in and out of Robinhood to external wallets, which was not always the case. Robinhood's crypto interface is simple — buy, sell, and hold.

Webull Crypto

Webull supports 45+ cryptocurrencies, giving you access to a wider range of altcoins. Webull also supports crypto transfers to external wallets. The charting tools for crypto are the same advanced tools available for stocks, which is a significant advantage for technical traders.

However, for dedicated crypto trading, neither app is the best option. Both charge spreads that are higher than dedicated exchanges.

Options Trading

Options trading is where both platforms offer real value compared to traditional brokerages. Zero commission and zero per-contract fees save active options traders hundreds of dollars per month.

Robinhood Options

Robinhood makes options accessible to beginners with a clean, visual interface. The options chain is easy to read, and the app provides clear breakeven prices, max profit, and max loss for every trade before you confirm. Robinhood supports covered calls, protective puts, spreads (vertical, iron condors, etc.), and basic multi-leg strategies.

Webull Options

Webull's options interface is more data-rich. You get Greeks (delta, gamma, theta, vega) displayed prominently, implied volatility charts, options flow data, and the ability to analyze strategies with a built-in profit/loss calculator. For intermediate and advanced options traders, Webull provides significantly more analytical depth.

Winner: Robinhood for beginners. Webull for intermediate/advanced options traders.

User Interface and Experience

Robinhood

Robinhood's interface is a masterclass in simplicity. The clean, minimalist design makes investing feel approachable. The home screen shows your portfolio value, daily gain/loss, and a watchlist. Buying a stock takes three taps. The learning curve is essentially zero — anyone with a smartphone can figure it out in minutes.

The downside of simplicity is that it can feel limiting. There is no desktop app (web only for desktop), limited customization, and the news feed is basic. Power users will quickly outgrow it.

Webull

Webull's interface is information-dense. The home screen shows market indices, top movers, sector heat maps, economic calendars, and news. The stock detail pages show financials, analyst ratings, institutional ownership, short interest, and insider trading — all for free. The desktop app is a full-featured trading terminal.

The downside is complexity. New investors may feel overwhelmed by the amount of data on screen. Webull has a steeper learning curve, and the mobile app can feel cluttered compared to Robinhood's clean design.

Winner: Robinhood for simplicity and beginners. Webull for data and power users.

Cash Management and Banking

Robinhood

Robinhood has evolved into a financial super-app. Robinhood Gold members earn 4.5% APY on uninvested cash (1.5% for free users). The Robinhood Gold credit card offers 3% cashback on all purchases, deposited directly into your investment account. There is also a debit card with ATM fee reimbursements and a checking/savings-like experience within the app.

Webull

Webull offers 5.0% APY on uninvested cash, which is higher than both Robinhood's free (1.5%) and Gold (4.5%) tiers. However, Webull does not offer a credit card, debit card, or the broader banking features that Robinhood provides.

Winner: Robinhood for the ecosystem (credit card, debit card, cash management). Webull for the highest cash APY.

Free Stock Offers

Robinhood

New users receive 1 free stock valued between $5 and $200 upon sign-up. No deposit required. Referring friends earns additional free stocks. The stocks are randomly selected from a pool, with most landing in the $5-$10 range.

Webull

Webull's sign-up bonus is more generous. New users can receive up to 12 free stocks when they open an account and make an initial deposit. The value ranges from $3 to $3,000 per stock. Deposit tiers determine how many stocks you receive — depositing more earns more free stocks.

Winner: Webull, with a significantly more generous sign-up bonus.

What Neither App Tells You

Both Robinhood and Webull earn a significant portion of their revenue through payment for order flow (PFOF). This means they sell your order data to market makers like Citadel Securities and Virtu Financial. While this subsidizes your "free" trading, it can result in slightly worse execution prices.

For small trades (under $10,000), the PFOF cost is negligible — typically less than a penny per share. For larger trades, consider using a broker like Interactive Brokers that offers direct market access and price improvement.

Both platforms have also faced scrutiny. Robinhood restricted trading of GameStop (GME) and other meme stocks in January 2021, drawing massive backlash and a Congressional hearing. Webull also restricted trading during the same period but received less attention. Both have since made improvements to their risk management and communication.

Our Final Verdict

Both Robinhood and Webull are excellent free trading apps, but they excel at different things:

Choose Robinhood if you:

  • Are new to investing and want the simplest possible experience
  • Want an all-in-one financial app (investing + banking + credit card)
  • Want an IRA with a 1% employer-style match
  • Prefer clean, minimalist design over data density
  • Want the lowest margin rates (5.75% with Gold)

Choose Webull if you:

  • Want professional-grade charting and technical analysis for free
  • Are an active trader who relies on indicators and chart patterns
  • Want extended pre-market hours (4 AM ET)
  • Want paper trading to practice strategies risk-free
  • Want the most generous sign-up bonus (up to 12 free stocks)
  • Want a wider selection of cryptocurrencies (45+)

The smartest move is to open both accounts. Claim the free stocks from each, use Webull for analysis and charting, and use Robinhood for its cash management and IRA match. There is no rule that says you can only use one brokerage.
